Introduction

Wars are no longer fought only on battlefields: today most conflicts take place in the streets, in people’s homes and in the daily lives of ordinary citizens.
Conflict & Human Suffering is a data-driven exploration of how war and violence affect not only politics and geography, but especially human lives, those of innocent people whose only fault is being born in the wrong place.
Through visual storytelling we tell the numbers of pain, the attacks, the victims, the protests, but also the hope that arises in the darkest moments.
Every story we share combines data analysis and human perspective, transforming statistics into narratives that help us understand not only what happens during conflicts, but who happens to.

This project is for them.
