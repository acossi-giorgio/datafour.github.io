Attacks and abductions: violence against civilians does not stop
Annual number of events against civilians by type (“attack” and “abduction or forced disappearance”).

The histograms show how the number of attacks and abductions has changed over the years in Syria and Palestine.
We observe a divergent trend in Syria: while direct attacks have gradually decreased in recent years, cases of abduction and forced disappearance have increased. This change suggests a transformation in the dynamics of the conflict: violence has become more silent, aimed at greater control over the population.
In Palestine, on the other hand, the number of attacks has increased over time, with a clear peak during the period of greatest tension. Abductions, by contrast, are concentrated only in two specific periods, 2020 and 2024, highlighting isolated but significant episodes.
