Now let's look at the topics covered in the first chapter from a temporal perspective.
The graphs show the evolution of the main forms of violence: political violence events, protests, events targeting civilians and civilian fatalities.
We observe that, while most other countries have remained stable or even decreased, Palestine has seen a tragic increase in violent events and attacks against civilians in recent years. This increase is also reflected in a significant rise in civilian fatalities.
In the case of Yemen, on the other hand, there has been an increase in protests over the last year. This trend could reflect a period of particular political or social instability in the country. The other states, on the contrary, show more stable levels or no significant changes.
Comparing these trends allows us to understand not only the significance of individual episodes, but also the deeper dynamics that characterise the evolution of conflicts over time.
