Moving from the number of attacks to the actual number of victims, the tragedy becomes even clearer. The numbers fluctuate, but in some years they explode. Each peak in the charts corresponds to waves of attacks, offensives, or bombings that indiscriminately affect the population. The visualizations expose the human dimension of the conflict. The graphs show the differences between countries, but also a sad common trend: the loss of civilian life continues to grow over time, concentrated in areas already devastated by war.

From the streets comes the response to violence

As the blood flows, so does the resistance. Protests, both peaceful and violent, are increasing across almost all countries. It is proof that the population does not remain helpless in the face of violence: society, despite taking many risks, is looking for a space to make its voice heard. The streets have become the other face of the conflict.
