The Complexity of Modern Conflicts
The composition of conflicts

This Sankey diagram illustrates the anatomy of conflict by mapping the flow from primary event categories to specific sub-events for a selected country and time period. The width of each stream corresponds to the frequency of incidents, immediately highlighting the most dominant forms of violence, while color coding differentiates the nature of each event. This visualization reveals the underlying structure of instability, showing how broad patterns of unrest, armed conflict, and violence against civilians translate into specific actions on the ground.
