The Complexity of Modern Conflicts
Visualize the flow from event types to sub-event types

The Sankey diagram offers a clear overview of how different forms of violence unfold within a specific country and time range. Each flow connects broad event categories to their more detailed sub-events, showing how general patterns branch into concrete actions on the ground. The thickness of the lines highlights where violence is most concentrated, while the colours help distinguish the nature of each event. Together, these streams form a visual pathway through the conflict: a way to understand how various forms of unrest, attacks, and civilian harm are distributed and transformed over the years.
